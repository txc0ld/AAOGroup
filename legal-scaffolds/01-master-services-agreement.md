## 2. Background

- AAO designs, deploys and operates AI-assisted operational workflows for Australian small and medium businesses, with human approval gates and Australian-resident data handling.
- The Client wishes to engage AAO to deliver one or more such workflows under a Statement of Work (**SOW**) attached to this Agreement.
- This Agreement sets the master commercial terms. Each SOW sets the specific scope, fees and timeline for an engagement.

## 7. AI Limitations and Approval Responsibility

> ***⚠️ counsel: this clause is the single most important commercial protection for AAO. Please verify enforceability of the "Approver bears responsibility for the final action" position under the Australian Consumer Law and any sectoral regulations relevant to the Client.***

7.1. The Client acknowledges and agrees that:

- (a) AI-generated Outputs are **drafts**. They are produced by large language models (**LLMs**) that can make errors, omit context, or generate plausible-sounding content that is incorrect (**hallucination**).
- (b) Every Output that affects an external party, a financial transaction, or a regulated decision is routed to an Approver before it is sent or actioned, in accordance with the Approval Matrix in the SOW.
- (c) The **Approver is responsible** for reviewing each Output and making the final decision to approve, edit, reject or escalate. AAO does not exercise the Client's professional judgement on the Client's behalf.
- (d) AAO does not warrant the accuracy, suitability or legal correctness of any Output. Where an Approver approves an Output, the Client adopts that Output as its own.
- (e) The categories of decision listed in the **AI Limitations Disclosure** (Schedule 4) are **prohibited uses** of the Service.

7.2. AAO will:

- design Workflows so that material decisions are gated by Approval;
- maintain Audit Logs of every Output and Approval;
- run a monthly governance review of Workflows in production; and
- notify the Client of any incident in accordance with clause 11.

## 8. Data Handling

8.1. AAO handles Client data in accordance with the **Data Processing Schedule** (Schedule 2).

8.2. Without limiting Schedule 2:

- (a) AAO will not use Client data to train any AI model. AAO will only engage Sub-processors that contractually commit to the same.
- (b) Where the Client has selected sovereign residency for a Workflow, AAO will use Sub-processors and infrastructure regions in Australia for that Workflow. Where a Workflow requires processing outside Australia, this will be documented in the SOW and the Client's prior written agreement obtained.
- (c) AAO will apply the AAO Data Sensitivity Classification (Schedule 2) to determine handling rules for each data class in a Workflow.
- (d) Default retention periods are set in Schedule 2 and may be adjusted in the SOW.

## 9. Intellectual Property

9.1. **Client data**. The Client owns all data the Client provides or that flows through Integrations. AAO has a limited licence to use that data only to deliver the Service.

9.2. **Workflow configurations**. The configuration files, prompts, and policies created for the Client's specific Workflows are owned by the Client on payment of the relevant SOW fees, subject to clauses 9.3 and 9.4. ***⚠️ counsel: confirm that handing IP in the configuration to the Client (as opposed to licensing it) is the right commercial position. The alternative — perpetual non-exclusive licence — may better protect AAO's reuse across clients.***

9.3. **AAO IP**. AAO retains all rights in:

- (a) the AAO framework, methodology, skills library, prompt templates, guardrail patterns, and operational runbooks;
- (b) any pre-existing software, tools or libraries used to deliver the Service; and
- (c) any improvements to (a) or (b) developed in the course of delivering the Service.

9.4. **Anonymised learnings**. AAO may use anonymised, aggregated learnings from Workflows (e.g. patterns of failure modes) to improve the AAO framework. AAO will not include any Client identifier or Client Personal Information in such learnings, and will obtain the Client's prior written consent before using any learning that could be identifying. ***⚠️ counsel: confirm "consent" is the right standard here vs. "notification".***

## 10. Confidentiality and Security

10.1. Each Party will keep the other's Confidential Information confidential and use it only to perform this Agreement.

10.2. AAO will maintain security controls including:

- access control with multi-factor authentication;
- encryption in transit and at rest;
- least-privilege role-based access;
- audit logging of administrative actions;
- secure software development practices;
- annual third-party security review (report available on request); and
- background checks for personnel with access to Client data.

10.3. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for **5 years**, except that obligations regarding Personal Information continue for as long as required by law.

## 11. Incident Notification

11.1. AAO uses the following severity scale:

| Severity | Definition | AAO acknowledgement target |
|---|---|---|
| **S1** | Confirmed exposure of Client or end-customer data; security breach; unauthorised action sent externally. | **Within 1 hour** of detection |
| **S2** | Workflow failure that resulted in an action being sent (incorrectly or to the wrong recipient). | Within 4 hours |
| **S3** | Workflow failure caught by an Approval gate (no external action). | Next business day (AWST) |
| **S4** | Minor issue, no operational impact. | Within 5 business days |

11.2. For S1 incidents, AAO will:

- notify the Client's primary contact within **1 hour** of detection;
- provide a written incident summary within **24 hours**;
- provide a root cause analysis within **5 business days**; and
- where the incident involves Personal Information and meets the threshold under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme, support the Client to notify the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) and affected individuals.

11.3. ***⚠️ counsel: confirm whether AAO should commit to notifying OAIC directly, or only support the Client to do so. Position depends on whether AAO is treated as the APP entity or as a service provider to the Client APP entity.***

13.1. Fees for each engagement are set in the relevant SOW. Unless the SOW says otherwise:

- pilot fees are invoiced per the schedule in the SOW (typically 50% on signing, 50% on Acceptance);

13.2. All fees are **exclusive of GST**. AAO will issue tax invoices in accordance with *A New Tax System (Goods and Services Tax) Act 1999*.

13.3. Payment terms are **14 days** from the date of the tax invoice.

13.4. AAO may charge interest on overdue amounts at **2% per annum above the Reserve Bank of Australia cash rate**, calculated daily.

13.5. ***⚠️ counsel: confirm 14-day terms and overdue interest rate are commercially appropriate and enforceable.***

## 14. Term and Termination

14.1. This Agreement starts on the Effective Date and continues until terminated under this clause.

14.2. Each SOW runs for the **Initial Term** stated in it. Subscription SOWs continue **month-to-month** after the Initial Term until terminated by either Party on **30 days' written notice**.

14.3. Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately by written notice if the other Party:

- (a) commits a material breach and does not remedy it within 14 days of written notice;
- (b) becomes insolvent, enters administration or has a controller appointed; or
- (c) commits a serious or repeated breach of the Acceptable Use Policy or Schedule 4 prohibited uses.

14.4. AAO may suspend the Service immediately if continued operation poses a clear risk to the Client, end customers, or AAO's other clients, with notice to the Client as soon as practicable.

14.5. On termination, the offboarding process at Schedule 7 applies.

## 15. Liability

> ***⚠️ counsel: this clause is critical. The cap of "12 months fees paid" is a common SaaS position but may need to be higher (or have specific carve-outs) for engagements that touch financial systems or customer data. Please review against the Client's risk profile.***

15.1. Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits liability that cannot be excluded or limited by law, including liability under the Australian Consumer Law for guarantees that cannot be excluded.

15.2. Subject to clause 15.1 and 15.4, each Party's total aggregate liability under this Agreement is capped at the greater of:

- (a) **AUD $50,000**; or
- (b) the **fees paid or payable by the Client to AAO under the relevant SOW in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim**.

15.3. Subject to clause 15.1, neither Party is liable for any indirect, consequential, special or punitive loss, loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of goodwill, or loss of data (other than the cost of restoring data from the most recent backup AAO is required to hold).

15.4. The cap in clause 15.2 does not apply to:

- (a) the Client's obligation to pay fees;
- (b) breach of confidentiality;
- (c) wilful misconduct or fraud; or

## 16. Indemnities

16.1. **AAO indemnifies the Client** against third-party claims that the Service infringes Australian intellectual property rights, provided the Client:

- promptly notifies AAO;
- gives AAO sole control of the defence; and
- provides reasonable assistance.

16.2. **The Client indemnifies AAO** against claims arising from:

- the Client's data being unlawful or infringing third-party rights;
- the Client's use of an Output in breach of clause 7 or Schedule 4;
- the Client's breach of the Acceptable Use Policy.

## 17. Force Majeure

17.1. Neither Party is liable for failure to perform (other than payment) due to events beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, pandemics, government action, internet or telecommunications failures, or third-party LLM provider outages, provided the affected Party promptly notifies the other and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate.

17.2. If a force majeure event continues for more than 30 days, either Party may terminate the affected SOW on written notice.

## 18. Dispute Resolution

18.1. The Parties will first try to resolve any dispute by good-faith negotiation between senior representatives within **14 days** of written notice of the dispute.

18.2. If unresolved, the Parties will refer the dispute to mediation in Perth under the rules of the Resolution Institute, before commencing court proceedings.

18.3. This clause does not prevent either Party seeking urgent injunctive relief.

## 19. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

19.1. This Agreement is governed by the laws of **Western Australia**.

19.2. The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Western Australia.

## 20. General

20.1. **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's written consent (not unreasonably withheld). AAO may assign to a related entity or successor on notice.

20.2. **Notices.** Notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses in clause 1 or to the email addresses nominated in the SOW. Notices to AAO regarding legal matters must also be sent to **legal@aaogroup.au**.

20.3. **Entire agreement.** This Agreement (with its Schedules and SOWs) is the entire agreement and supersedes prior discussions.

20.4. **Severability.** If any clause is unenforceable, the rest remains in force.

20.5. **No waiver.** A failure to enforce a right is not a waiver of that right.

20.6. **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including by electronic signature.

20.7. **Survival.** Clauses 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 13 (for accrued amounts), 15, 16, 19 and any clause that by its nature should survive, survive termination.
